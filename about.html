Our Mission

The Halley Project was created with a simple yet ambitious goal: to become the world's premier destination for accurate, accessible, and engaging information about Halley's Comet. We believe that this celestial phenomenon, which has captivated humanity for millennia, deserves a dedicated platform that combines scientific accuracy with educational accessibility.

Why This Project Exists

As the next return of Halley's Comet approaches in 2061, we recognized the need for a centralized, reliable source of information. Many existing resources are scattered, outdated, or overly technical. The Halley Project bridges this gap by providing:

  • Real-time countdown to the next perihelion
  • Historical context and cultural significance
  • Practical observation guides for amateur astronomers
  • Educational resources for teachers and students
  • A community platform for comet enthusiasts

Open Source & Community Driven

The Halley Project is an open-source initiative. We believe that knowledge about our universe should be freely accessible to all. Our codebase is publicly available, and we welcome contributions from developers, astronomers, educators, and enthusiasts worldwide.

Whether you're a professional astronomer with data to share, a developer who wants to improve our tools, a translator who can help us reach more people, or simply someone passionate about comets, there's a place for you in The Halley Project.
